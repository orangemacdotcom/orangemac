🚀 Getting Started with Slice

Adding Your First Feed

To start reading, you need to add an RSS feed:

  1. Tap the + button in the top-right corner
  2. Paste your feed URL (e.g., https://example.com/feed)
  3. Tap Subscribe
  4. Your feed articles will appear immediately
💡 Tip: Most websites have an RSS feed link in their footer. Look for an RSS icon or link labeled "Subscribe."

Finding Feeds to Subscribe To

Use the Discover tab to explore popular feeds organized by category. Tap any feed to preview articles, then subscribe with one tap.

Managing Your Feeds

In the Feeds tab, you can:

  • Drag to reorder feeds
  • Swipe left to save articles
  • Swipe right to mark as read
  • Long-press for more options

✨ Key Features

📁Categories

Organize your feeds into custom categories to keep your reading organized and easy to browse.

⭐Favorites

Star your favorite feeds for quick access. They appear in a dedicated Favorites tab.

🔖Save Articles

Bookmark articles to read later. Access all saved articles from the Saved tab.

🔍Search

Search across all your articles by title, content, author, or feed name.

🎨Themes

Choose between Light, Dark, or System theme. Pick your accent color from presets or customize.

📱Alternate Icons

Change your app icon from Settings. Choose between Original and Dark variants.

🎙️ Podcast Features

Playing Podcasts

When you subscribe to a podcast feed, episodes appear in your feed list. Tap an episode to play it:

  • Playback continues in the mini-player while you browse
  • Tap the mini-player to expand to full player view
  • Use lock screen controls to pause, skip, and adjust speed

Playback Controls

  • Play/Pause: Main button in the center
  • Skip Back 15s: Left skip button
  • Skip Forward 30s: Right skip button
  • Speed Control: Tap speed indicator (1×, 1.5×, 2×, etc.)

Smart Squeeze

Smart Squeeze automatically detects and speeds up silent pauses in podcasts, letting you listen faster without missing content.

Toggle it on/off in Settings → Features → Smart Squeeze

Auto-Resume

Slice remembers where you left off in each podcast episode. When you return to an episode, it automatically resumes from the last position.

⚙️ Settings & Preferences

Features Section

  • Smart Stitch: Reads full articles beyond RSS summaries
  • Privacy Minder: Removes HTML embeds, videos, and tracking
  • Smart Squeeze: Speeds up podcast silences
  • Infinity Reading: Automatically load the next article

Reading Preferences

  • Font Size: Adjust text size for comfortable reading
  • Article Images: Toggle images in articles
  • Compact Mode: Reduce spacing for more articles per screen
  • Mark as Read on Open: Auto-mark articles when viewed

Item Types

Toggle which content types to show:

  • Show Articles: Display text articles from feeds
  • Show Podcasts: Display podcast episodes from feeds

You can hide podcasts to see only articles, or vice versa.

Keyword Filters

Hide articles containing specific words or phrases. Add filters in Settings → Reading → Keyword Filters.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How often does Slice refresh feeds?

By default, Slice uses iOS background refresh to periodically update your feeds. The frequency depends on your usage patterns and iOS settings. You can manually refresh any time by pulling down on the feed list.

Can I import my feeds from another reader?

Yes! If you have an OPML file from another RSS reader:

  1. Go to Settings → Data & Storage → Import OPML
  2. Select your OPML file
  3. Slice will import all feeds and subscriptions

How do I export my feeds?

To back up your feeds or move to another reader:

  1. Go to Settings → Data & Storage → Export OPML
  2. Select where to save the file
  3. Share or save your OPML file

What's the difference between read and unread?

Articles are unread by default. Once you open an article (if "Mark as Read on Open" is enabled) or manually mark it as read, it becomes read. Read articles appear grayed out and don't count toward your unread badge.

How do I clear cached data?

In Settings → Data & Storage, you can:

  • View how much space each feed uses
  • Swipe on a feed to clear its cached articles
  • Clear all saved articles

Can I read offline?

Slice caches articles locally, so you can read previously downloaded articles offline. New articles require an internet connection to download.

Does Slice support audio in the background?

Yes! Podcasts will continue playing when you lock your phone or switch apps. Use lock screen controls or the mini-player to pause/play.

How do I report a bug or request a feature?

We'd love to hear from you! Contact us at support@orangemac.com with detailed information about the issue or feature you'd like to see.

🔧 Troubleshooting

Feeds not updating

Solution:

  • Check your internet connection
  • Manually refresh by pulling down on the feed list
  • Try removing and re-adding the feed if the URL changed
  • Check if the feed is muted (settings icon on the feed)

Articles showing old content

Solution: The article may not have full content in the RSS feed. Try:

  • Enable Smart Stitch in Settings to fetch full articles
  • Tap "Open in Browser" to read the full article on the website

Podcast not playing

Solution:

  • Check internet connection (for streaming)
  • Ensure volume is not muted
  • Try closing and reopening the app
  • Check that the feed is actually a podcast with audio enclosures

App using too much storage

Solution:

  1. Go to Settings → Data & Storage
  2. See which feeds use the most space
  3. Clear caches for large feeds
  4. Reduce the article limit to keep fewer articles cached

Search not finding articles

Solution: Search only works on articles you've already downloaded. Try:

  • Increase the article limit in Settings
  • Manually refresh feeds to download more recent articles
  • Make sure articles are not filtered (unread-only mode)

Still having issues?

If you're experiencing problems not listed here:

  1. Try force-closing and reopening the app
  2. Check that you're running the latest version
  3. Clear app cache if possible
  4. Contact support with details about your issue
